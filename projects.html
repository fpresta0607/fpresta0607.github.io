Development of a Customized Epileptic Monitoring System

I am proud to be part of a cutting-edge collaboration with the Neurosurgery department at the Medical College of Wisconsin (MCW). Our goal is to design and implement a custom EEG recording system that will revolutionize the invasive monitoring of epileptic patients. Our team is dedicated to meeting the specific needs of both the patients and the department, ensuring mechanical adaptability, pristine signal processing, and dependable memory storage. Under the leadership of clinical researcher, Dr. Hernan Rey, we are conducting innovative experiments involving the implantation of microelectrodes into patients with epilepsy. These patients undergo a series of stimulation tests over several days, allowing us to acquire valuable data 24/7. As a key member of this project, I am developing a custom GUI to monitor critical device information, as well as optimizing an ETL pipeline to streamline the data acquisition process. With the successful implementation of these innovative solutions, we can ensure the reliability and efficiency of our data collection efforts, further propelling this groundbreaking research forward
